### About Me

I am a former visiting student at the University of Oxford, where I studied Computer Science and Mathematics. My research at OMNI is supervised by [Dr. Ana Namburete](/members/ana-namburete), based in the [Department of Computer Science](https://www.cs.ox.ac.uk). My home institution is [Columbia University](https://www.cs.columbia.edu/), where I am completing my final year of a B.A. in Computer Science with a minor in Mathematics.

My research focuses on applying computer vision and machine learning techniques to fetal brain imaging. Specifically, I am fine-tuning and extending the [SAM2](https://ai.meta.com/research/sam2/) model to segment anatomical structures in fetal brain ultrasound videos using a small number of annotated frames. This approach aims to significantly reduce the annotation effort required for downstream 3D fetal brain tasks.

### Research Background

In addition to my work at OMNI, I have held research positions at Stanford Medicine and Columbia University. At Stanford Medicine's [Aghaeepour Lab](https://nalab.stanford.edu/), I worked under the supervision of [Dr. Nima Aghaeepour](https://nalab.stanford.edu/team/nima-aghaeepour/) and [Dr. Tomin James](https://nalab.stanford.edu/team/tomin-james/) on explainable machine learning methods for chorioamnionitis prediction, developing attention-based architectures to better understand model decision-making over time-series clinical data. At Columbia University's [Sia Laboratory](https://sialab.squarespace.com/), I worked under the supervision of [Dr. Samuel Sia](https://www.bme.columbia.edu/faculty-staff/directory/sam-sia) and [Terry Chern](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=7fQS4zIAAAAJ&hl=en) to develop a dashboard that enables physicians to efficiently monitor patients with hypertension remotely.
